Lloyd Comiter

Lloyd completed his undergraduate studies at Boston University and earned his law degree at St. Thomas University School of Law in Miami. Lloyd was admitted to the Florida Bar in 1991 and is a member of the United States District Court, Southern District of Florida, General Bar, the United States Court of Appeals for the Eleventh Circuit, the Supreme Court of the United States and the United States District Court, Northern District of Florida.

Lloyd is a member of the Palm Beach County Bar Association where he has been the Chair of the Small Claims Court Clinics Committee since 2007 and the South Palm Beach County Bar Association. He is a Florida Supreme Court Certified County Court Mediator where he volunteers in county court meditating small claims court and landlord/tenant disputes. Lloyd is also a Florida Supreme Court Certified Circuit Court Mediator and Florida Supreme Court Certified Family Mediator. Lloyd is an Approved Dispute Resolution Center, Supreme Court of Florida, County Mediation Assistant Trainer. Lloyd is also an Approved Dispute Resolution Center, Supreme Court of Florida, Circuit Mediation Subject Matter Specialist.

Lloyd previously served on two Grievance Committees (serving in the Chair capacity as well) which are composed of lawyers and nonlawyers responsible for continuing the investigation of lawyer misconduct referred by Florida Bar discipline attorneys. Additionally, Lloyd served on the Florida Bar Small Claims Rules Committee for 12 years and served as Chair of this Committee as well. Lloyd served on the Judicial Campaign Practices Commission for the 2012, 2014 and 2016 judicial campaign election cycle and is a former Civil Traffic Hearing Officer.

Lloyd has lived in South Florida since 1967 and is married to Meryl and they have three children.
